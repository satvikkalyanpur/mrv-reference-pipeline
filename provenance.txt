--- AGB EQUATION (Areca catechu) ---
Equation used (combined, best fit): AGB(kg/plant) = 0.03883 * H * D^1.2 ; R^2 = 0.96
  H = palm height (m), D = stem diameter (cm)
Alternative forms from same source:
  Height-only:   AGB = 0.816 * H^1.42 ; R^2 = 0.89
  Diameter-only: AGB = 0.0689 * D^2.59 ; R^2 = 0.74
SOURCE: Prayogo, C. et al. (2018) "Allometric Equation for Pinang (Areca catechu)
  Biomass and C Stocks." AGRIVITA Journal of Agricultural Science, 40(3): 381-389.
  DOI: 10.17503/agrivita.v40i3.1124

--- DESK-POC HONESTY STATEMENT & LIMITATION ---

Scope:
This work is a reference-implementation demonstration on open and proxy data. It is
not a validated carbon measurement and does not issue credits. It has no field
validation, no physical soil cores, and no ground-truth tree inventory. Species,
DBH, height, and tree count are assumed inputs; only the equation forms and
coefficients are drawn from cited sources.

Limitation 1 Ś AGB equation:
The areca allometric equation (Prayogo et al. 2018) was derived in Papua, Indonesia 
from a small destructive sample and is not India-specific. An Indian areca equation
(e.g. Bhat et al., Environmental Challenges, 2021) is preferred if retrievable, with
Prayogo cited as supporting evidence.

Limitation 2 Ś BGB ratio:
No areca- or palm-specific root-to-shoot ratio was found in the literature searched.
Because palms have fibrous, adventitious roots unlike trees, the tree-derived default
(0.24, IPCC 2006) is used only as a provisional proxy and is treated as a key stated
uncertainty.

Limitation 3 Ś SOC change:
The SOC-change values are illustrative only. They come from a conservative
literature-based rate, not a field-calibrated model, and must be replaced by a
calibrated soil model before any credit issuance.
