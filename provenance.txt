--- METHODOLOGY FRAMEWORK ---
Basis: VM0047 Afforestation, Reforestation and Revegetation, v1.1
  Status: active since 14 May 2025 (Verra VCS).
  Succeeds CDM AR-ACM0003 (effective 28 Sep 2023), cited as lineage.
  Pools: above-ground and below-ground biomass; deadwood, litter, and soil
    organic carbon are optional. Soil organic carbon is excluded here, as
    permitted by VM0047; biomass is restricted to live woody biomass.
  Uncertainty: minimum 10% deduction, expressed at the 90% confidence interval
    as a percentage of the estimate; a higher deduction may be selected.
  Permanence: buffer pool via the VCS AFOLU Non-Permanence Risk Tool
    (risk-rated).
  Verifier: Validation and Verification Body (VVB).
India CCTS (offset mechanism): agroforestry is a Phase-1 offset sector
  (Detailed Procedure, 27 March 2025), but the agroforestry methodology is not
  yet approved and no numeric quantification rules (uncertainty deduction,
  buffer, pool treatment) are published. CCTS is therefore treated
  qualitatively and is not used to compute credits. Verifier: Accredited
  Carbon Verification Agency (ACVA).

--- ABOVE-GROUND BIOMASS EQUATION (Areca catechu) ---
Combined form (best fit): AGB(kg) = 0.03883 * H * D^1.2 ; R^2 = 0.96
  H = palm height (m); D = stem diameter (cm)
Alternative forms (same source):
  Height-only:   AGB = 0.816 * H^1.42 ; R^2 = 0.89
  Diameter-only: AGB = 0.0689 * D^2.59 ; R^2 = 0.74
Source: Prayogo, C. et al. (2018). "Allometric Equation for Pinang
  (Areca catechu) Biomass and C Stocks." AGRIVITA Journal of Agricultural
  Science, 40(3): 381-389. DOI: 10.17503/agrivita.v40i3.1124
Note: the equation is derived from a destructive sample in Indonesia; an
  India-specific arecanut equation would be preferable where available.
  Inputs are assumed to fall within the equation's calibration range.

--- CARBON FRACTION ---
Values compared: 0.47 and 0.50
Source: 0.47 = IPCC 2006 Guidelines Vol.4 (AFOLU) default; 0.50 = palm-carbon
  literature.

--- ROOT-TO-SHOOT RATIO (BGB = AGB * RSR) ---
Values compared: 0.15 and 0.24
Source: 0.15 = Pragasan (2025), arecanut carbon stock assessment, Coimbatore;
  0.24 = IPCC 2006 Guidelines Vol.4 tropical default (tree-derived).
Note: a palm-specific root-to-shoot ratio is not well established; palm root
  architecture differs from trees, so this parameter carries genuine
  uncertainty, which this analysis quantifies.

--- UNCERTAINTY DEDUCTION ---
Values compared: 10%, 15%, 20%
Source: VM0047 v1.1 requires a minimum 10% uncertainty deduction (90%
  confidence interval); a higher deduction may be applied.

--- PERMANENCE BUFFER ---
Values compared: 10%, 20%, 40%
Source: VCS AFOLU Non-Permanence Risk Tool (risk-rated); representative low,
  medium, and high values used.

--- CO2e CONVERSION ---
Value: 3.667 (= 44/12), the CO2-to-carbon molecular-weight ratio.

--- STAND PARAMETERS (assumed; measured in operational deployment) ---
~1370 palms/ha (~2.7 m x 2.7 m arecanut spacing);
D = 12 cm (stem diameter, approximately constant for palms);
H = 10.0 to 10.4 m (height growth ~0.4 m/yr).
Held fixed across the sensitivity analysis, which varies methodology choices
only.

--- INTEGRITY / AUDIT ---
SHA-256 hash chain over key results (each record incorporates the previous

--- FINAL RESULTS ---
Baseline issued: 0.817 tCO2e/yr (CF=0.47, RSR=0.24, uncertainty=10%, buffer=20%)
Sensitivity (36 combinations): 0.505 - 0.978 tCO2e/yr | spread 1.94x | CV 17.9%

--- SCOPE STATEMENT ---
This is a reproducible reference-implementation demonstration on open data.
It is not a field-validated measurement: there are no soil cores and no
ground-truth tree inventory. Tree dimensions, count, and species are assumed
inputs; equation forms, coefficients, and methodology rules are cited. Soil
organic carbon is excluded under VM0047's optional-pool provision. India's
CCTS is treated as context and is not used to compute credits.
