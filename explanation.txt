========================================================
MRV SENSITIVITY ANALYSIS — EXPLANATION GUIDE
========================================================

--------------------------------------------------------
1. WHAT THIS IS
--------------------------------------------------------
A reproducible, open-data pipeline that takes one arecanut agroforestry parcel
and shows that its carbon-credit total is not a single fixed number — it varies
depending on which defensible methodology choices are made. The pipeline runs
the credit calculation for every combination of reasonable choices and reports
how much the issued-credit number varies. The calculation uses VM0047, the
current international afforestation/reforestation standard. India's CCTS is
discussed as context, because CCTS has not yet published the numeric rules
needed to compute agroforestry credits.
--------------------------------------------------------
2. THE FINDING
--------------------------------------------------------
For the same 1.27 ha parcel, issued credits range from 0.51 to 0.98 tCO2e/yr
across 36 defensible parameter combinations — a 1.94x spread (CV 17.9%).
Methodology choices that are each individually permitted can nearly double or
halve the credit outcome for identical trees. Because India's CCTS has not
fixed these parameters for agroforestry, a CCTS agroforestry credit outcome is
currently indeterminate.
--------------------------------------------------------
3. WHAT EACH OUTPUT MEANS
--------------------------------------------------------
Mean NDVI (0.873): confirms dense live vegetation over the parcel. NDVI is used
only to confirm canopy, not to estimate biomass.
Map: true-colour, NDVI, and boundary — the site figure for the paper.
Baseline issued credits (0.817 tCO2e/yr): the result under one reasonable set
of choices — what a conventional single-answer tool would report.
Sensitivity range (0.505-0.978, spread 1.94x): the core result — how much that
single answer moves across all defensible choices.
Tornado figure: which parameter moves the credit number most (typically the
uncertainty deduction and permanence buffer) — the main results figure.
VM0047 vs CCTS table: what each framework specifies, and where CCTS is silent.
Hash chain: tamper-evidence over the key results.
Summary: the headline line for the abstract and results.
--------------------------------------------------------
4. WHY SOIL ORGANIC CARBON IS EXCLUDED

--------------------------------------------------------
Under VM0047, soil organic carbon is an optional pool; when it is excluded,
biomass carbon is restricted to live woody biomass. It is excluded here on this
methodological basis. In practice, projects frequently exclude soil carbon
because measuring it credibly costs more than the credit value it adds.
--------------------------------------------------------
5. WHERE CCTS IS ADDRESSED IN THE PAPER
--------------------------------------------------------
Abstract: one line — CCTS has opened agroforestry crediting but has not
defined the rules.
Introduction: the problem in full — no approved agroforestry methodology and
no numeric quantification rules published.
Methods: VM0047 is used as a proxy framework because CCTS is not yet
computable.
Discussion: credits vary by ~2x on parameters CCTS has left undefined, so
CCTS agroforestry credit outcomes are currently indeterminate.
--------------------------------------------------------
6. SCOPE (what this does and does not claim)
--------------------------------------------------------
- Not field-validated: no soil cores, no measured trees; open/proxy data.
- Tree diameter, height, count, and species are assumed inputs (measured in
operational deployment); equations, coefficients, and rules are cited.
- Species is not detected from satellite; arecanut is assumed from regional
cropping patterns.
- Credits are not computed under CCTS; CCTS has no numeric rules yet.
- The contribution is the reproducible, applied demonstration and the open
tool — not a claim that methodology choice affecting credits is a new
discovery.
--------------------------------------------------------
7. CONTRIBUTION (one line)
--------------------------------------------------------
A reproducible, open-data demonstration of how much smallholder agroforestry
carbon credits vary with defensible methodology choices under VM0047, and a
timely observation that India's CCTS has left exactly these parameters
undefined for agroforestry.
